Map Tests

Interactive maps with cached tiles

  • More details than most map tile vendors provide. Compare to Google Maps and bing.
  • A less colorful map style, and to make it easier to see overlays.
  • Many business listings, so if a robbery occurs next to the 'Apple Store' on that map, a search for 'robbery at Apple Store' will find it
  • Building addresses
  • Polygons from shapefiles (OPD Beats)
  • Large sets of polygons and points from shapefiles (Miami Dade Police Grids and Station Locations)
  • Ability to handle very large data sets, like this layer of over 10 million crimes that Spotcrime relased.
  • Very large data sets - 330,000 License Plate Readings over 2 months. When zoomed in, it shows the individual license plates.
  • Heat Maps
  • Shaded clusters, that show a lot more information than the heat maps
  • Buffers around polygons - such as the 1000 foot School Zones described in the Federal Gun Gree School Zone law
  • Using such buffers to filter data, such as these LPR readings that occurred within school zones.
  • US Census Department zip codes.
